Different Types of Phishing

Phishing comes in various types, with different strategies employed by scammers. Below is a brief description of each phishing type.

Phishing: This broad term for email-based exploits involves hackers tricking targets into clicking a link or opening a malicious attachment to gain access to highly sensitive data, passwords, or bank account and credit card details.

Spear Phishing: These are emails sent only to a select few, often customized to deceive the recipient into opening them with their personal information. It arises through the Internet.

Clone Phishing: This is a type of attempted attack whereby the legitimate email address and email address are cloned, after which another message is sent from that lookalike address with structurally different contents or attached malicious files.

Whaling Phishing, BEC and Pretexting: Cybercriminals attempt to trick high-ranking executives, including CFOs and CEOs, into providing a wire transfer to a fraudulent account or using an employee to direct a wire transfer to help with fraud.
