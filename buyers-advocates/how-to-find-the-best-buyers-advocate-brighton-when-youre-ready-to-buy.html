Get preapproved for a mortgage

Many home shoppers hire a buyer advocate and explore their home-shopping options without talking to a mortgage lender first. Yet, it is a wise idea to speak with a mortgage lender first to receive a pre-approval amount, which will help when buying a home. The pre-approval for a mortgage lets you know the maximum limit you're willing to borrow and provides potential realtors and sellers info that you intend on making an offer, thus encouraging them to work in your favour.

Get referrals from friends and family

Invite people to your network for suggestions. It may be helpful to find someone with whom you're likely to share similar home-buying experiences. First-time buyers differ from repeat buyers and eventually, sellers onboarding downsize, so for a buyer advocate who has experience working with a similar diversity of clients, it's ideal.

Research potential candidates

Make sure to take a look at an advocates online presence and reviews. Look through the advocate s website and social media. Spend some time looking at the advocate's online reviews as well. Categorize one or occasional negative opinions as nothing to be alarmed about, but more than that could be a warning. 

Examine your state's buyer agency to find out whether the local buyer broker you're considering is certified by your state and doesn't have any complaints on its record. Compile the list of buyer brokers you're considering and review them on forums or other places to find out whether any complaints have been filed against them.

Interview at least three buyer advocates

An interview is your chance to get a feel for the advocate's style and experience. Ultimately, you're interested in someone who is knowledgeable about a particular area and understands your finances and needs. Questions to ask about a buyer advocate include how long they have been doing business and describe the number of buyers and sellers they have worked with in the past. You should also be sure to receive information regarding the price range of buyers and sellers, how they help buyers and sellers stay competitive on the market, and how much they charge for their commission.

Additionally, interviews give you the chance to understand an advocate's communication style and how well they work around your schedule.

Request references — and check them

Ask your buyer advocate to inquire about previous customers he's ready to offer you references so that you can assess the kind of service he offers. It is often beneficial to get in touch with those clients to gauge their experience and the level of assistance the advocate provided during the process, including during negotiations.

Go with your gut

Equally important as an advocate's knowledge and years of experience is their capacity to help you navigate the home-buying process smoothly. Regardless of whether or not things get rocky along the way, just be sure to work with an advocate whose experiences you feel good about. There are a variety of other buyer advocates out there that will be a much better match for your personality and will be happy to assist you.

Conclusion

It’s smart to do your homework when shopping for a buyer advocate. Some advocates have handled deals in your neighbourhood, and while past performance does not guarantee future results, it can provide helpful information. A quality advocate is worth the cost, but look to distinguish one advocate from the next.
