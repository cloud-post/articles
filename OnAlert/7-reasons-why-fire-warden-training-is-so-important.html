7 Reasons Why Fire Warden Training is So Important

Fire Warden Training is an important and well-known profession, yet still many people do not realise just how important it is. The fire safety industry is a multi-million dollar industry, with a high number of jobs, a constant demand for new people, and a need for those who are trained in fire safety, fire protection and emergency response. There are many careers in this industry, and fire warden training is only the starting point. A career in the fire warden industry will give you a chance to get paid to help people and save lives in your community.

Prevention

Prevention is better than a cure! The fire wardens or wardens in your office aren't just in charge of responding to a fire emergency; they are also harnessed in proactive attempts to prevent one from happening.

1. Promoting Fire Safety in the Workplace

One strategy fire wardens use to reduce workplace fires is by promoting fire safety. A fire warden ensures that the other staff members and occupants of his workplace are aware of the dangers of potential fire sources that could develop in the office environment, such as electrical fires as well as fires that could possibly stem from the accumulation of debris.A sign can be erected in these areas with combustible material or electrical hazards, instructing staff on fire safety procedures, and ensuring that all occupants are aware of basic fire safety information.

2. Understanding the Building and Fire Equipment

In the case of a fire emergency, a fire warden always needs to know the location of the floor or building they're in, the location of exits, and the location of fire extinguishers and communication systems. Not only that, but a fire warden is responsible for the upkeep of fire extinguishers in the building.

3. Running Fire Drills and Evacuation Simulations

One way to deal with the normal chaos that occurs during an emergency is making sure all personnel, children and building occupants are ready. Simulation fire and evacuation exercises are valuable for fire wardens to practice using their training, gather information about surrounding areas, and instruct other staff members about what they'll need during a real emergency.

4. Understanding Fire Risk Control Measures

Fire wardens are responsible for educating workers on the installation, preparation and detection of fire hazards in the workplace.It is critical that fire equipment and emergency exits remain readily accessible, flammable materials are stored correctly, and any potential risks are assessed and reviewed.

During an Emergency

When smoke is sensed or a fire alarm system is activated, the need for fire alarm training becomes critical at the office. Aside from sounding the alarm, fire alarm training enables a warden to take part in a variety of actions and emergency procedures to ensure everyone is secure.

5. Acting as a Leader in the Chaos

A fire warden has a crucial responsibility in the event of a fire. It is absolutely one of the most difficult jobs for a firefighter to possess, as people often go into shock or become vulnerable by being evacuated from their structures. A building fire warden must be a chief, able to successfully guide everyone out of the building using the safest pathways. They must be sure to examine each room to ensure everyone has evacuated safely.

In the event of an evacuation, a fire warden is responsible for helping anyone who is  mobility-impaired to safely evacuate the location. Once the firefighters have checked the building for all persons, the assembly area must be checked for safety. When the head count checks out, the fire alarms shall be activated.

6. Operating Fire Equipment

Fire warden training provides fire wardens with fire-fighting equipment including fire extinguishers, fire blankets, and fire extinguisher reels.

7. Communicating with Emergency Services

Briefing any relevant emergency services when they arrive can be crucial in command decisions that emergency personnel make. Fire wardens must quickly inform firefighters of where the fire started, what type of fire it was and whether there may be any civilians in the area.

Conclusion

Although it is one of the most important changes that you can make in your facility, it is also one of the most overlooked. You can improve the safety of your building and the lives of your employees by including fire warden training in your facility.
